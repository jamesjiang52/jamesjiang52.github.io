Introduction

Welcome!

To borrow from Seinfeld, this is a website about nothing. I created this site because I wanted to try my hand at front-end web development. Clearly, this project is still in its early stages, and much more content and improvement is yet to come.

About the Author

First of all, I'll talk a little bit about myself. My name is James, and I was born in Japan, in a suburb of Tokyo. I moved to Burnaby, BC, Canada when I was three, and attended high school there. Now, I'm living in Toronto, ON.

I'm currently in my first year of undergraduate post-secondary study at the University of Toronto. I'm enrolled in the Computer Engineering program, and right now, I'm super interested in machine learning and its applications (which is why I'm also planning to do a minor in Artificial Intelligence in my upper years). My other computer-related interests include, among others, quantum computing and large-scale super computing. I absolutely love programming (Python 3 is my favorite language, by far), though admittedly my personal projects are a bit lacking. In my upper years, I plan to enrol in courses pertaining to both computer hardware and software, in order to expose myself to everything that modern computing has to offer. My dream job, as of now, is at Intel Corporation.

Aside from that, music is another one of my deep interests. I play the piano, the B♭ soprano clarinet, and am currently learning how to play guitar. On piano, I absolutely adore pieces from the Romantic era, especially the works of Chopin. (His nocturnes are, in my opinion, the most brilliant pieces that were ever composed.) On clarinet and guitar, in addition to the classical genre, I also play jazz and contemporary compositions. As for listening, my favorites are classical, of course, though I'll also listen to jazz, as well as soundtracks from my favorite movies and video games (as odd as that may seem).

This provides a great segway into my third major interest: gaming. For me, gaming is an escape from the real world, analogous to a good novel. One of my favorite games of all time is a single-player open-world role-playing game by Bethesda Game Studios, known as The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, which satiates my desire for exploration. This game is exquisitely crafted, and, no doubt, much of the content on this site will pertain in some way to Skyrim. Even the name of this website was inspired by a particular quest from the game that involves the retrieval of an exceedingly powerful ancient artifact.

Content

In terms of the actual content of this site, as I mentioned in the introduction, there isn't a method to this madness. I'll probably be posting code to some of my side projects, as well as writing blog posts to update my progress on them. I might also post some pictures or photographs of stuff, and maybe even upload a few videos and soundtracks. Who knows?

However, I'd like to keep this site distinct from a "personal portfolio"-type website. This is just a place where I'll post things that I think are worth posting (and probably a few things that aren't), whether or not they're relevant to my career path or not.

All the source code for this webpage can be found on my GitHub.
